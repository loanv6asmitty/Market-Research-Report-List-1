<p><p>The automotive LiDAR system market comprises two primary types: mechanical LiDAR and solid-state LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR uses rotating components to scan the environment, providing high-resolution 3D mapping but requiring more space and being susceptible to wear. In contrast, solid-state LiDAR employs fixed components and relies on electronic scanning, resulting in a more compact design, enhanced durability, and potentially lower manufacturing costs. Both types are crucial for advancing autonomous vehicle technology and enhancing safety features.</p></p>
