<p><p>The food container market is primarily divided into flexible and rigid packaging. Flexible packaging includes materials like plastic films, pouches, and bags that adapt to the food's shape, offering lightweight and space-saving options. It often enhances shelf life and reduces waste. In contrast, rigid packaging consists of containers such as jars, bottles, and cans that provide structural support and protection. This type is ideal for products requiring durability and long-term storage. Both types cater to diverse food preservation and marketing needs.</p></p>
