<p><p>The medical grade hyaluronic acid market is primarily categorized into two types: animal tissue-derived and microbial fermentation-based products. Animal tissue-derived hyaluronic acid is extracted from sources like rooster combs and is traditionally used for medical applications, including joint injections and dermatological procedures. In contrast, microbial fermentation produces hyaluronic acid through bacterial fermentation, offering a more sustainable and consistent alternative, often preferred for its purity and absence of animal-derived components. Each type serves diverse medical applications, catering to various consumer needs.</p></p>
